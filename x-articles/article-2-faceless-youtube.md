### Scripting

**ChatGPT / Claude** – Write outlines, research, draft scripts
- Cost: $20/month for premium

**Your Brain** – You still need to edit and add personality
- Cost: Free (hopefully)

**Pro tip:** AI writes the first draft. You add the hooks, the personality, the parts that make people actually watch. Never post raw AI output.

### Voiceover

**Option 1: Your Voice**
- Cost: Free
- Pros: Unique, builds brand, no ongoing costs
- Cons: Takes time, requires decent mic ($100-200)

**Option 2: ElevenLabs AI Voice**
- Cost: $22/month (Creator tier)
- Pros: Consistent, fast, multiple voices available
- Cons: Can sound slightly artificial, some viewers hate AI voices

**Option 3: Hire a VO Artist**
- Cost: $50-200 per video
- Pros: Human quality, professional
- Cons: Expensive at scale, scheduling delays

**My recommendation for starting:** Use ElevenLabs. It's good enough now that most viewers won't notice. Graduate to human VO when you're profitable.

**DaVinci Resolve:** Free, more powerful, steeper learning curve

**Canva:** For thumbnails ($13/month for Pro)

## The Step-by-Step Beginner Guide

Here's exactly how I'd start a faceless channel from zero in 2026.

### Week 1: Research & Setup

**Day 1-2: Pick Your Niche**
- Watch 50+ videos in potential niches
- Note what gets views, what doesn't
- Check CPM estimates (Social Blade, creator interviews)
- Pick ONE niche. Don't try to do multiple.

**Day 3-4: Channel Setup**
- Create Google account (separate from personal)
- Set up YouTube channel
- Design basic branding (logo, banner) in Canva
- Write channel description with keywords

**Day 5-7: Study the Winners**
- Find 10 successful channels in your niche
- Analyze their:
  - Title formats
  - Thumbnail styles
  - Video lengths
  - Hook structures
  - Content sources

### Week 2-4: Create Your First 10 Videos

**Why 10?** Because your first videos will suck. Everyone's do. You need reps to get good. And YouTube needs data to understand your channel.

**Video Creation Process:**

1. **Research** (1-2 hours)
   - Find your topic/story
   - Gather all source material
   - Outline the narrative

2. **Script** (2-3 hours)
   - Write hook (first 30 seconds = most important)
   - Write full script
   - Edit for pacing—cut everything boring

3. **Voiceover** (30 min - 1 hour)
   - Record or generate with ElevenLabs
   - Edit out mistakes, pauses

4. **Visuals** (2-4 hours)
   - Gather footage
   - Edit in CapCut/Resolve
   - Add text overlays, effects
   - Match visuals to script

5. **Thumbnail** (30 min)
   - Design in Canva
   - Test different styles
   - Make it POP

6. **Upload & Optimize** (30 min)
   - Write title (include keywords)
   - Write description
   - Add tags
   - Schedule or publish

**Total time per video: 6-10 hours initially**

This gets faster. By video 20, you'll be at 3-5 hours. By video 50, maybe 2-3 hours.

### Month 2-3: Consistency & Learning

- Post 3-4 videos per week minimum
- Study your analytics obsessively
- Double down on what gets views
- Kill what doesn't work
- Engage with comments (builds community, signals algorithm)

### Month 4+: Scale or Die

By now you should know:
- What titles work for your niche
- What thumbnails get clicks
- What content style keeps viewers watching
- Where to find content reliably

**If you're seeing growth:** Keep going. Consider hiring help.

**If you're flat:** Analyze what's working for competitors but not for you. Pivot if needed.

## The Stuff Nobody Talks About

### Copyright Will Wreck You If You're Dumb

Using clips from movies, TV shows, music? **You will get claimed or struck.**

Safe content:
- Stock footage
- Your own recordings
- Creative Commons material
- Very short clips with commentary (Fair Use—but risky)
- Court footage, bodycam (usually public domain)

**Don't risk your channel over a movie clip.** It's not worth it.

### Burnout Is Real

Posting 3-4 videos per week is exhausting. Most people quit before monetization.

Solutions:
- Batch create (film/record 5 videos in one day)
- Build systems (templates, workflows)
- Hire help as soon as you can afford it
- Take breaks before you hate your channel

### The Algorithm Owes You Nothing

You can make a great video and get 200 views. That's just how it works.

The game is:
- Volume (more videos = more chances)
- Iteration (get better over time)
- Patience (6-12 months minimum commitment)

If you're not willing to post 100+ videos before judging results, **don't start.**
