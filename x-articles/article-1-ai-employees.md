## Meet Hunter: My Lead Generation Machine

Hunter is my first AI employee. His job is simple but critical:

**Find leads before anyone else does.**

Here's what Hunter does, 4 times every single day:

1. Scans Craigslist "gigs" section for construction-related posts
2. Filters out the garbage (lowballers, scams, unrealistic projects)
3. Extracts the good ones with contact info
4. Scores them based on urgency and fit
5. Sends me a summary with the best opportunities

**Hunter works at 6 AM. 12 PM. 6 PM. And midnight.**

While my competitors are checking Craigslist once a day—maybe—Hunter has already flagged the hot leads and I'm reaching out before they even see the post.

Let me give you real numbers.

Last month, Hunter found 47 potential leads. After filtering, 23 were worth reaching out to. I landed 8 jobs from those.

**8 jobs I would have missed** if I was manually checking Craigslist like everyone else.

The kicker? Hunter costs me about **$12/month** to run. That's the API costs for the AI processing and automation tools.

$12/month for a tireless lead generation system that never sleeps.

You know what a human lead gen assistant costs? Minimum $2,000/month if they're any good. And they definitely won't work at midnight.

## Meet Steve: My Operations Manager

Steve is more sophisticated than Hunter. Steve is basically my right hand.

Here's what Steve handles:

**Morning Briefings**

Every single morning at 7 AM, Steve sends me a brief to my phone. Not an email I'll ignore. A direct message that wakes me up with everything I need to know:

- Today's scheduled jobs and crew assignments
- Weather forecast (critical in construction)
- Any client messages that came in overnight
- Pending estimates that need to go out
- Upcoming deadlines

I literally wake up knowing exactly what my day looks like. No scrambling. No "oh shit I forgot about that job."

**Proposal Writing**

This is where Steve really shines.

Client sends me job details? Steve drafts a professional proposal. Not a template with blanks filled in—an actual customized proposal that references their specific project, timeline, and requirements.

I review it, maybe tweak a few things, and send it out. What used to take me 45 minutes now takes 5.

**Scheduling Coordination**

Steve keeps track of my calendar, crew availability, and project timelines. When a new job comes in, Steve can tell me exactly when I can fit it in without double-booking or overcommitting.

**Client Communication**

Steve drafts follow-up messages, sends appointment reminders, and keeps clients in the loop on project status. Professional, timely, consistent.

You know what most contractors are terrible at? Communication. Clients hate it when you go dark for three days.

Steve makes sure that never happens.

## How You Can Start Today

You don't need to wait for me to build your system. You can start right now.

Here's my advice:

**Step 1: Identify Your Biggest Time Sink**

What admin task eats most of your day? Lead generation? Estimates? Follow-ups? Pick ONE thing.

**Step 2: Learn One AI Tool**

Start with ChatGPT if you haven't already. Learn how to write prompts that get useful outputs. Practice with your actual business problems.

**Step 3: Automate One Thing**

Use Zapier, Make, or n8n to connect your tools. Set up ONE automation. Lead comes in → get a notification. Simple.

**Step 4: Iterate**

Your first system will be janky. That's fine. Improve it. Add to it. Make it better over time.

**The goal isn't perfection. The goal is starting.**

Six months ago, Hunter was a terrible script that barely worked. Now he's finding me 8+ jobs per month.

It all started with deciding to try something different.
