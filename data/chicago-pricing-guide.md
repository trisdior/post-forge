# Valencia Construction — Chicago Market Pricing Guide
**Last Updated: February 2026**

---

## How To Use This Guide

This document contains **market-rate pricing for the Chicago metropolitan area** as of early 2026. All figures represent realistic ranges that Valencia Construction can use for estimating, quoting, and benchmarking jobs.

**Key principles:**
- All prices are presented as **ranges** (low to high) to account for neighborhood variation, job complexity, material grade, and access conditions.
- **Labor and materials are included** in quoted ranges unless explicitly noted otherwise.
- Chicago runs **10-15% above national averages** due to stricter building codes, older housing stock, higher labor rates, and seasonal constraints.
- The **Completed Jobs Log** at the bottom of this document should be filled in after every job. Over time, this real data will sharpen estimates and reveal which job types are most profitable.
- When quoting, start with these ranges, then adjust for site-specific conditions (parking, stairs, building age, lead/asbestos risk, permit requirements).
- Markup and overhead guidance is provided in Section 12. Apply it consistently.

## 9. PLUMBING BASICS

### Important: Chicago Licensed Plumber Requirements

**Chicago Municipal Code (Chapter 18-29) requires that ALL plumbing work be performed by or under the supervision of a City of Chicago licensed plumber.** This includes:
- Any work on water supply lines, drain lines, vent stacks, or gas lines
- Fixture installations that involve modifying supply or drain connections
- Water heater installation or replacement
- Adding new plumbing fixtures or relocating existing ones

**What Valencia CAN do without a plumber's license:**
- Replace a faucet (same location, no pipe modification)
- Replace a toilet (same location, standard wax ring swap)
- Replace a garbage disposal (same location)
- Re-caulk around fixtures
- Replace shut-off valves (simple compression fittings)
- Snake a drain (basic clearing)

## 10. ELECTRICAL BASICS

### Important: Chicago Licensed Electrician Requirements

**Chicago Municipal Code requires that ALL electrical work be performed by a City of Chicago licensed electrician.** Chicago does NOT recognize the state of Illinois electrical license — the electrician must hold a **City of Chicago Supervising Electrician License** or work under one. This includes:
- Any new circuits, wiring runs, or panel work
- Adding outlets or switches
- Upgrading electrical service/panels
- Hardwired appliance connections (dishwasher, range, HVAC)
- Any work that requires a permit

**What Valencia CAN do without an electrician's license:**
- Swap a light fixture (like-for-like, existing box, no new wiring)
- Replace a switch or outlet (existing wiring, same type)

### Chicago Basement Notes

- **Waterproofing first.** Chicago's clay soil and high water tables mean moisture is the #1 issue. Never finish a basement without addressing moisture. Budget $2,000-$8,000 for moisture remediation on most older homes.
- **Permit required.** City of Chicago requires a building permit for basement finishing. Include $500-$1,500 in estimates.
- **Egress code.** Any sleeping room in a basement must have an egress window or door. Budget $2,500-$5,500 per window.
- **Ceiling height.** Chicago code requires minimum 7 ft finished ceiling height in habitable spaces. Measure before quoting — many older basements are marginal.
- **Radon.** Common in Chicagoland. Recommend testing ($150-$250) and mitigation system ($800-$1,500) if levels are elevated.

## 12. OVERHEAD & MARKUP

### Standard Markup Structure

| Component | Rate | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| **Materials markup** | 15-25% | Over wholesale/contractor cost. Standard 20%. |
| **Subcontractor markup** | 10-15% | Applied to sub invoices. Standard 10-12%. |
| **Overhead (operating costs)** | 10-18% | Insurance, vehicle, tools, phone, software, marketing, accounting. |
| **Profit margin** | 10-20% | Target net profit after all costs. |
| **Combined markup (overhead + profit)** | **30-40%** | Applied to total estimated cost (labor + materials + subs). |

### How to Apply

**Example: Job with $5,000 in estimated direct costs (labor + materials + subs)**

| | Low (30%) | Target (35%) | High (40%) |
|---|-----------|-------------|------------|
| Direct costs | $5,000 | $5,000 | $5,000 |
| Markup | $1,500 | $1,750 | $2,000 |
| **Customer price** | **$6,500** | **$6,750** | **$7,000** |

### When to Use Each End of the Range

| Scenario | Markup |
|----------|--------|
| Competitive bid, simple scope, repeat customer | 30% |
| Standard job, reasonable scope | 35% |
| Complex job, tight access, old building, high-rise, rush timeline | 38-40% |
| Emergency / after-hours / weekend premium | 40%+ or add flat surcharge |

### Contingency

| Job Type | Contingency to Build In |
|----------|------------------------|
| Cosmetic / straightforward (painting, flooring, handyman) | 5-10% |
| Moderate complexity (bathroom refresh, kitchen refresh) | 10-15% |
| Full remodel / gut renovation | 15-20% |
| Old construction (pre-1950), unknown conditions | 20-25% |

**Always disclose contingency handling to customer** — either build it into the quote as a buffer or present it as an explicit allowance line item.
