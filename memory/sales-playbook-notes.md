## Core Philosophy

**The 15-Minute Rule:** First to respond wins. 78% of customers choose the first contractor who responds.

**Pipeline Over Chaos:** 6-stage structured process:
1. **New Lead** → Log immediately
2. **Qualified** → Budget/timeline/authority confirmed
3. **Consultation** → In-home visit scheduled
4. **Proposal Sent** → 3-tier options delivered
5. **Follow-Up** → 14-day nurture sequence
6. **Closed/Lost** → Won or documented reason

### Within 15 Minutes of Lead:
1. **Call** first (leave voicemail if no answer)
2. **Text** immediately after call
3. **Email** formal intro + calendar link

- "I'd love to see the space in person—does [tomorrow] or [day after] work better?"

### Text Template:

## Qualification Questions (Save Time)

Ask BEFORE scheduling consultation:
1. **Budget:** "Most [kitchen] remodels we do run $20K-60K. Is that in range for you?"
2. **Timeline:** "When are you hoping to start?"
3. **Decision-maker:** "Will anyone else be involved in the decision?"
4. **Property:** Own or rent?
5. **Previous quotes:** "Have you talked to other contractors?"

**Red Flags to Decline:**
- "Just looking for a ballpark" (no real intent)
- Budget under $5K for major remodel
- "My landlord will pay" (no authority)
- Comparison shopping 10+ contractors

## In-Home Consultation (The Closer)

### Phase 1: Rapport (5-10 min)
- Compliment something specific ("Love the exposed brick!")
- Ask about home/neighborhood
- Find common ground
- **Goal:** Be memorable, likable

### Phase 2: Discovery (15-20 min)
**Money Questions (ask early):**
- "What's your budget range for this?"
- "Have you set aside funds or exploring options?"

**Pain Questions:**
- "What's frustrating you most about this space?"
- "What's not working in your daily routine?"
- "If you could wave a wand, what would this look like?"

**Priority Questions:**
- "What's most important: speed, cost, or quality?"
- "Are there any must-haves vs. nice-to-haves?"

### Phase 3: Measure & Inspect (10-15 min)
- Take detailed photos
- Measure everything (bring laser measure)
- Check for hidden issues (water damage, electrical, structural)
- Narrate what you see: "I'm noticing X, which means Y"

### Phase 4: Next Steps (5 min)
- "I'll have a detailed proposal to you by [specific day]"
- "It'll include 3 options so you can choose what fits best"
- Get phone number/email confirmation
- **Ask for referral NOW:** "Do you know anyone else who might need work done?"

## 3-Tier Proposal Strategy

Every proposal includes 3 options:

### Tier 1: Essential ($)
- Bare minimum to solve the problem
- Budget-friendly
- Anchors the low end

### Tier 2: Recommended ($$)
- What you'd actually suggest
- Best value/quality balance
- **Where most people land**

### Tier 3: Premium ($$$)
- All the bells and whistles
- Luxury finishes, extended warranties
- Makes Tier 2 look reasonable

**Example - Kitchen Remodel:**
- Essential: $22K (basic cabinets, laminate counters, builder-grade appliances)
- Recommended: $38K (semi-custom cabinets, quartz counters, mid-range appliances, tile backsplash)
- Premium: $58K (custom cabinets, granite/marble, high-end appliances, designer lighting)

**Delivery:** Email + follow-up call same day. "Just sent over your proposal—do you have 5 minutes to walk through it?"

## 14-Day Follow-Up Sequence

**Day 1:** Send proposal + immediate call  
**Day 2:** Text: "Did you get a chance to review?"  
**Day 3:** Email: Case study of similar project  
**Day 5:** Call: "Any questions I can answer?"  
**Day 7:** Text: "Still interested? Happy to adjust options"  
**Day 10:** Email: Financing options if budget is concern  
**Day 14:** Final call: "Should I close this out or adjust something?"

**After Day 14:** Move to "Lost" pipeline, but add to quarterly check-in list.

**Key Insight:** 80% of sales happen after 5+ touchpoints. Most contractors give up after 1-2.

## Objection Handling

### "I need to think about it"
- "Totally understand. What specifically do you need to think about—budget, timeline, or scope?"
- (Isolate the real concern, address it directly)

### "You're too expensive"
- "Compared to what? Did another contractor quote lower?"
- "I can adjust the scope—what's your target budget?"
- Show value: "Here's what you're getting that others don't include..."

### "I want to get 2-3 more quotes"
- "Smart move. What will you be comparing—price, timeline, or materials?"
- "When you get other quotes, make sure they include [X, Y, Z] so it's apples-to-apples"
- (Stay helpful, not defensive)

### "I'm not ready to start yet"
- "No problem. When are you thinking?"
- "Want me to check back in [month]?"
- Add to CRM for future follow-up

## Closing Techniques

### Assumptive Close
- "When would you like us to start—next week or the week after?"
- "I'll get you on the schedule. Do mornings or afternoons work better?"

### Urgency Close
- "We have a crew finishing up next Friday. If you book this week, we can start Monday"
- "Material prices go up March 1st—if we lock in now, I can hold current pricing"

### Referral Close
- "If you're happy with the proposal, I'd love to earn your business. Can I ask—who referred you to us?"
- (If no referral: "Would you feel comfortable referring us to a neighbor after we knock this out?")
