## Part 2: SMS Sequence (Hours 0-24)

### SMS 1: Immediate Response (Within 30 min of lead)
**Best time:** 8:00 AM - 8:00 PM (same day lead posted)

```
Hi [Name]! Saw your post about [kitchen/bathroom/etc]. Licensed & insured Chicago contractor, tons of remodels. Free estimate. Reply YES to set up a quick call. -Valencia Construction
```

**Key elements:**
- Personalized (acknowledges their specific need)
- Proof (licensed, insured, experience)
- Value (free estimate)
- CTA (clear, easy response)
- Signature (builds familiarity)

**Why SMS first?**
- 98% open rate within 3 minutes
- Fastest way to capture attention
- Feels personal (not automated)
- Easy to reply from phone

**Response templates (based on reply):**
- "YES" → "Perfect! I'm free 10-11am or 2-3pm today. Which works? -Tris"
- "Tell me more" → "What's your project? Kitchen, bathroom, flooring? Budget range? -Tris"
- No response after 2h → Move to SMS 2

---

### SMS 2: Social Proof + Urgency (4 hours later)

**Time: 12 PM if morning lead, or 8 PM if afternoon lead**

```
One quick thing — most homeowners wait too long and regret it. Prices on materials go up, contractors get booked. If you're serious about this year, let's talk this week. Free estimate. Reply or call 773-682-7788 -Tris
```

**Key elements:**
- Creates mild urgency (wait → regret)
- Social proof (contractors book up)
- Multiple channels (call option)
- Window (this week)

---

### SMS 3: Last Touch (24 hours later, if no response)

```
Last message — I work with homeowners just like you. Kitchen remodel? $25K-50K. Bathroom? $15K-30K. Free estimate, no pressure. Call me 773-682-7788 or reply. -Tris
```

**Key elements:**
- Acknowledges silence (respecting their time)
- Price anchoring (sets expectations)
- Removes friction (no pressure)
- Direct phone number (bypass messaging)

## Part 5: Call Script (When They Reply "YES" or Call)

**Goal:** Book a consultation within 2 weeks

### Opening (0-30 seconds)
```
Hi [Name], this is Tris from Valencia Construction. Thanks for getting back to me. 

You're looking at a [kitchen/bathroom/flooring] project, right? 

[Wait for response]

Perfect. Just so you know, I do this myself or oversee it closely — not a big company, just me and my crew. That's why we're usually cheaper and faster than other contractors.

Does that sound like what you're looking for?
```

### Qualification (30 sec - 2 min)
```
Cool. Quick questions so I give you accurate info:

1. Is this project happening this year?
2. Do you have a budget range in mind? (Or should I tell you what typical projects cost?)
3. Kitchen, bathroom, or something else?
4. What's your main concern — cost, timeline, or quality?
```

### Value Add (2-3 min)
```
[Based on their answers, customize:]

"Most people in your situation spend between $[X] and $[Y]. Here's what that includes...

What's important to you is [timeline/cost/quality]. Let me tell you how we handle that differently than other contractors..."

[Share 1-2 specific examples of similar projects]
```

### Close to Consultation (3-4 min)
```
Here's what I'd suggest:

I can come to your place, take measurements, see what we're working with, and give you a detailed estimate with options. No charge, no pressure.

We can do [2-3 time options]. What works for your schedule?

[If no immediate availability:]
"What's your preferred week? I'll send you a couple time slots and you pick."
```

### Objection Handling

**"I need to get other quotes first"**
→ "Absolutely, that's smart. Get 2-3 quotes. When you do, call me back and let me know. I bet my price is competitive and my timeline is faster."

**"I'll call you back when I'm ready"**
→ "Good plan. Here's my direct line: 773-682-7788. Text me when you're ready and I'll pencil you in. Most people find that having a solid quote on hand makes the decision easier."

**"Your price seems high"**
→ "I might be higher than someone else. We usually are — because we finish on time and everything works. Most clients realize they saved money overall because we don't have cost overruns or do it twice."

**"Can you just give me a ballpark?"**
→ "I could, but it would probably be wrong. Every space is different. That's why I want to see it in person. 20 minutes, and I'll have a real number for you."
