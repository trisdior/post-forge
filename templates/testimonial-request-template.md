## What to Do With Testimonials

**Once you collect them:**

1. **Get permission** — Make sure they're okay with you using their name and testimonial publicly
2. **Capture details** — Note their project type (kitchen, bathroom, etc.), location, and any specific praise
3. **Store them** — Create a `testimonials.md` file organized by project type and date
4. **Use them strategically:**
   - Website homepage (rotate 3–4 top testimonials)
   - Google Business Profile
   - Facebook/Instagram posts
   - Email signature
   - Proposal documents (show social proof)
   - Paid ads (if they're detailed enough)

---

## Pro Tips for Getting Better Testimonials

**Do:**
- Ask specific questions (not just "how was it?")
- Ask early (within 1-2 weeks of completion while experience is fresh)
- Make it easy (email, text, voice note — their choice)
- Thank them genuinely
- Follow up if they don't respond the first time

**Don't:**
- Write the testimonial for them and ask them to sign it (feels fake)
- Ask when they're stressed or emotional
- Pressure them or be pushy
- Wait months to ask (they'll forget)

**Bonus:**
- Ask permission to take before/after photos
- If they're on social media, tag them when you post their testimonial
- Send them a small thank-you gift ($25 gift card to a local restaurant)
