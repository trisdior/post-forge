## The YC Playbook (What EVERY billion-dollar YC company did)

### 1. TALK TO USERS FIRST
- Don't build in a vacuum. Talk to 50 potential customers before writing code.
- Paul Graham: "Make something people want" — literally YC's motto

### 2. DO THINGS THAT DON'T SCALE
- Airbnb founders went door-to-door photographing apartments
- DoorDash founders personally delivered food
- Stripe founders installed their product FOR customers ("Collison Installation")
- YOU: Manually run PostForge for 10 businesses. Learn what they actually need.

### 3. LAUNCH FAST, ITERATE FASTER
- "If you're not embarrassed by v1, you launched too late"
- Ship weekly. Get feedback. Ship again.
- Perfect is the enemy of revenue.

### 4. FOCUS ON ONE METRIC
- Pick ONE number that matters. For PostForge: Monthly Active Users.
- Everything you do should move that number.

### 5. PRICING = VALUE CAPTURE
- Don't charge for features. Charge for outcomes.
- "We'll save you 10 hours/week" > "AI content generator"
- Stripe charges 2.9% because they capture value proportionally.

### 6. FOUNDER-MARKET FIT
- The best founders KNOW their market personally
- You (Tris) know construction. You know small business pain.
- That's an unfair advantage over Stanford CS grads building "AI tools."
