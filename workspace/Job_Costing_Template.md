# Job Costing Spreadsheet Template

## Overview
Professional job costing template for Valencia Construction Services. Use this to calculate accurate project costs, labor hours, material waste factors, and profit margins. Helps with quoting and post-project analysis.

---

## HOW TO USE THIS TEMPLATE

### Step 1: Project Details
Fill in the top section with basic project info. This automatically pulls into summaries.

### Step 2: Labor Breakdown
- **Labor Category**: Demolition, Framing, Electrical, Plumbing, Finishing, etc.
- **Hours Estimated**: Total hours needed
- **Hourly Rate**: Your rate ($45-65 typical, varies by skill)
- **Total Labor**: Automatically calculated

### Step 3: Materials Breakdown
- **Item**: Specific material (2x4 lumber, tile, fixtures, etc.)
- **Qty**: Quantity needed
- **Unit Price**: Cost per unit
- **Waste Factor**: Add 10-15% for construction waste
- **Total**: Automatically calculated

### Step 4: Subcontractor Work
- List any specialized work (electrical license, plumbing, HVAC, etc.)
- Get quotes from subs and plug in

### Step 5: Equipment & Misc
- Tool rentals, dump fees, permits, insurance allocation, etc.

### Step 6: Profit Margin
- Set your target markup (35-50% typical for custom remodels)
- System calculates final proposal price

## EXCEL FORMULA NOTES

If converting to Excel, use these formulas:

- **Labor Total**: `=C2*D2` (Hours × Rate)
- **Material Total w/ Waste**: `=C3*D3*(1+E3)` (Qty × Unit Price × (1 + Waste %))
- **COGS**: `=SUM(all labor, materials, subs, misc)`
- **Markup**: `=COGS * (Profit Margin / 100)`
- **Proposal Price**: `=COGS + Markup`
- **Actual Profit %**: `=(Final Price - COGS) / Final Price * 100`

## QUICK TIPS

1. **Always add waste factor** — construction rarely goes perfect. 10-15% is standard.
2. **Adjust labor rates by complexity** — basic work ($45/hr), skilled ($55/hr), specialist ($75+/hr)
3. **Get sub quotes in writing** — don't estimate their work
4. **Review actual vs. estimated monthly** — spot where you're off, adjust next quote
5. **Keep markup consistent** — 35-50% typical (higher for custom, lower for production volume)
6. **Client never sees COGS** — only the Proposal Price line
7. **Build in contingency** — unexpected issues happen (price it in via markup, or separate $500 allowance)
