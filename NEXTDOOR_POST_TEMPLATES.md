# Nextdoor Post Templates
## 30 Days of Ready-to-Post Content for Chicago Remodeling

**Why Nextdoor?**
- 90+ million members, hyper-local focus
- Contractors & home service providers rank #2 trusted profession on Nextdoor
- Neighbors actively asking for recommendations (30% of recommendations are for contractors)
- High intent: people asking "who did your kitchen remodel?" get answered by locals who paid for it
- Less saturated than Facebook groups for local contractors

**Strategy:** Post 3x per week (M/W/F). Rotate between advice, FAQ, case studies, and soft sells.

### Post 8 (Wednesday)
**Type:** Surprising fact / Engagement  
**Goal:** Start conversations

```
🤯 Something contractors don't always tell you:

The order of remodeling work MATTERS.

Most people think: "Demo first, then build."

But smart remodeling goes:
1️⃣ Plan everything (design, materials, timeline)
2️⃣ Order long-lead items (appliances, custom cabinets—can take 6-8 weeks)
3️⃣ Demo old space
4️⃣ Run electrical/plumbing to new locations
5️⃣ Frame/walls/paint
6️⃣ Install cabinets, countertops, flooring
7️⃣ Final touches

**Why?** If you demo first but your cabinets don't arrive for 8 weeks, you're living in a disaster zone.

If you order BEFORE demo, everything arrives just in time. Timeline cuts in half.

This is the difference between a 6-week project and a 12-week project.

Tris's pro move: Build the plan, then build the timeline. 📋

Have you remodeled before? What surprised you most?

#Remodeling #HomeRenovation #Contractor101 #ProTips

```
❓ Q: "When is the best time of year to remodel?"

A: Honestly? Whenever you're ready. But here's the reality:

**SPRING/SUMMER (busiest)**
✅ Good: Weather cooperates, contractors available
❌ Bad: 12-16 week wait times, higher prices, everyone's remodeling

**FALL/EARLY WINTER (sweet spot)**
✅ Good: Shorter wait times (4-8 weeks), contractors eager for work, potentially better pricing
✅ Good: Finish by spring, enjoy your new space all summer
❌ Bad: Weather can be unpredictable

**WINTER (slowest)**
✅ Good: Shortest wait times (1-2 weeks!), best negotiating position
❌ Bad: Weather issues, shorter daylight hours

**MY HONEST ADVICE:**
Don't wait for the "perfect" season. Wait until YOU'RE READY.

But if you're flexible? Fall remodeling = best timing. You'll be done by spring, and contractors aren't slammed.

When are you thinking?

## Usage Guide for Tris

**Posting Schedule:**
- **Monday:** Authority, education, or new idea
- **Wednesday:** How-to, FAQ, or controversial opinion (engagement)
- **Friday:** Social proof, case study, or community building

**Best Times:**
- **9-10 AM** (morning commute, coffee break)
- **12-1 PM** (lunch break)
- **6-7 PM** (evening scroll)
- Use Nextdoor's "Schedule Post" feature to post at optimal times

**Engagement:**
- Reply to every comment within 2 hours
- Ask questions to drive conversation
- Don't hard-sell in comments (builds trust instead)

**Rotation:**
Use the 3-week cycle above, then repeat. Modify based on:
- Seasonal topics (fall = "why remodel now?" content)
- Current projects (case studies from recent work)
- Neighbor questions (answer directly, then expand to post)

**Measure:**
- Track: Neighbors saved, views, DMs, leads from Nextdoor
- After 3 months, you'll know what works best
- Double down on high-engagement posts
