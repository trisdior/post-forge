Kitchen Remodeling FAQs

Common questions from Chicago homeowners about kitchen renovations.

How long does a kitchen remodel take?
Most kitchen remodels take 4-8 weeks depending on scope. A cosmetic refresh (counters, backsplash, cabinet painting) can be done in 2-3 weeks. A full gut renovation with layout changes typically takes 6-8 weeks. We'll provide a detailed timeline before starting.
How much does a kitchen remodel cost in Chicago?
Kitchen remodel costs vary based on scope, materials, and layout changes. A cosmetic refresh costs less than a full gut renovation with custom cabinets and premium finishes. We provide detailed written estimates upfront so you know exactly what to expect — no surprises, no hidden fees.
Do I need permits for a kitchen remodel?
Permits are required for electrical work, plumbing changes, and any structural modifications (like removing walls). Cosmetic updates like countertops and cabinet refacing don't require permits. We handle all permit acquisition for you — it's included in our service.
Can I use my kitchen during the remodel?
For cosmetic updates, you'll have partial kitchen access throughout. For full renovations, plan to set up a temporary kitchen elsewhere in your home. We'll work with you to minimize disruption and complete the project as quickly as possible.
Do you help with design and material selection?
Yes! We'll help you choose cabinets, countertops, backsplash, flooring, and hardware that fit your style and budget. We have relationships with local suppliers and can often get better pricing than retail stores.
What's your warranty policy?
We stand behind our work. All labor is warranted for one year after project completion. Manufacturer warranties on materials and appliances apply separately. If something isn't right, we'll make it right.
