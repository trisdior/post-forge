## Strategy: Getting Your First 10 Reviews

### Phase 1: Inner Circle (Reviews 1–5)
**Timeline:** This week

1. **Make a list** of everyone you've done work for — even small jobs, favors, family projects
2. **Text them personally** using the template above (text > email for response rate)
3. **Follow up once** if they don't respond in 2–3 days — "Hey just bumping this up, no worries if you can't!"
4. Friends and family who've seen your work can also leave reviews based on their experience

**Key:** Don't send a mass text. Personal, 1-on-1 messages get way better results.

### Phase 2: Recent/Current Clients (Reviews 5–8)
**Timeline:** Ongoing

1. **Ask at the walkthrough** — when the client is happiest, right after seeing the finished product. Say something like: "I'm really glad you love it. If you get a chance, a Google review would mean the world to me."
2. **Follow up with the text template** the same day
3. **Include it in the post-job email sequence** (Sequence C above)
4. Make it easy — always include the direct review link

### Phase 3: Systematic (Reviews 8–10+)
**Timeline:** Ongoing habit

1. **Build it into your process** — review request is a standard step after every completed job
2. **Add a QR code** to your business card or leave-behind that goes directly to the Google review page
3. **Respond to every single review** within 24 hours — personalized, not generic. This encourages others to leave reviews too.
4. **Never offer incentives for reviews** — against Google's policy and can get your profile penalized

### Pro Tips
- **Timing matters:** Ask within 24 hours of job completion when excitement is highest
- **Make it stupid easy:** Always provide the direct link — never say "find us on Google"
- **Respond publicly:** Thank every reviewer by name and mention something specific about their project
- **Don't batch:** Space out review requests so they don't all come in on the same day (looks suspicious to Google)
- **Quality > quantity:** A detailed review mentioning "kitchen remodel in Lincoln Park" is worth 10x a generic "great work!"
