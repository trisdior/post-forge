### Day 14 — Myth Busting
**Post:**
Contractor myth I want to bust: "You should always go with the lowest bid."

NO. The lowest bid usually means:
❌ They're cutting corners on materials
❌ They don't have proper insurance
❌ They'll hit you with change orders later

Get 3 quotes. Go with the one who's licensed, insured, detailed in their estimate, and actually communicates. The cheapest option almost always ends up being the most expensive.

### Day 16 — Education (Permits)
**Post:**
Quick PSA: in Chicago, you need permits for most remodeling work — especially anything involving plumbing, electrical, or structural changes. A contractor who says "we don't need permits" is either cutting corners or doesn't know what they're doing.

Permits protect YOU. They ensure the work is inspected and up to code. If unpermitted work causes damage, your insurance can deny the claim.

Always ask your contractor about permits. A good one will handle it for you.

🔴 Call a pro: Anything larger than 6 inches, water damage, multiple areas, or if you want it to look seamless
