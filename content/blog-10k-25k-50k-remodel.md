## 💰 The $10K Budget: The Smart Refresh

**Best for:** First-time homeowners, landlords prepping units, or anyone who wants a noticeable upgrade without going into debt.

Think of $10K as your "make it feel like a new house" budget. You're not gutting anything, but you're transforming the vibe.

### What $10K Gets You in Chicago

- **Full interior paint job** (3-bed/1-bath, ~1,200 sq ft) — We're talking quality paint like Benjamin Moore or Sherwin-Williams, proper prep work, clean lines. Not your landlord's one-coat special. *($3,000–$4,500)*
- **New flooring in main living areas** — Luxury vinyl plank (LVP) is king at this budget. Brands like LifeProof or COREtec give you that hardwood look without hardwood problems. Runs about *$4–$7/sq ft installed* for 500–800 sq ft. *($3,000–$5,000)*
- **Bathroom refresh** — New vanity, mirror, light fixture, fresh paint, maybe swap the faucet and add new hardware. You're keeping the tub and tile but everything around it looks brand new. *($1,500–$2,500)*
- **Handyman punch list** — New door handles, outlet covers, cabinet hardware, weather stripping, minor drywall patches. The little stuff that makes a house feel *finished*. *($500–$1,500)*

### Realistic Timeline
**2–3 weeks** from start to finish, depending on scheduling.

### Chicago-Specific Context
Labor in Chicago runs higher than the suburbs — that's just the reality of working in the city (permits, parking, insurance, cost of living). A $10K budget in Naperville might stretch 15–20% further, but you're also not living in Naperville, so there's that.

### Pro Tip
**Splurge on paint quality, save on fixtures.** Good paint lasts 8–10 years. That $200 "designer" faucet from a big box store works just as well as the $80 one — it just looks slightly fancier in the showroom.

## 💰💰 The $25K Budget: The Real Transformation

**Best for:** Homeowners ready to actually *change* a space. This is where you start getting the "wow, this looks like a different house" reactions.

$25K is the sweet spot. You've got enough to do something significant without cutting corners on quality.

### What $25K Gets You in Chicago

**Option A: Full Bathroom Remodel ($12K–$18K) + Extras**

- Complete gut and rebuild of one bathroom
- New tub or shower (tiled walk-in shower with subway or large-format tile)
- New vanity (36" or 48"), toilet, fixtures — mid-range brands like Delta, Kohler, or Moen
- Proper waterproofing (Kerdi or RedGard — this is where cheap contractors cut corners and you pay for it later)
- New exhaust fan, lighting, heated floors if you prioritize it
- Cement board, not green board (if your contractor suggests green board in a shower, run)
- Leftover budget for paint + flooring in another room or two

**Option B: Partial Kitchen Remodel ($15K–$22K)**

- Reface or replace cabinets (RTA cabinets like Shaker-style from a wholesale supplier keep costs down while looking clean)
- New countertops — quartz runs $50–$75/sq ft installed in Chicago. Granite is comparable. Laminate if you want to save and put money elsewhere.
- New backsplash (subway tile is timeless and affordable at $8–$15/sq ft installed)
- Updated fixtures and hardware
- New sink + faucet
- *Note: This does NOT include moving walls, relocating plumbing, or new appliances. Those are $50K territory.*

**Option C: Basement Finishing (~800 sq ft)**

- Framing, insulation, drywall
- LVP flooring (moisture-resistant — critical for Chicago basements)
- Basic bathroom rough-in or half bath
- Recessed lighting
- Egress window if needed for bedroom code compliance (Chicago requires this — budget ~$2,500–$4,000 for the window alone)

### Realistic Timeline
**4–8 weeks** depending on scope, permits, and inspections. Chicago permit timelines are... let's just say "unpredictable." Budget an extra 1–2 weeks for the city to do its thing.

### Chicago-Specific Context
Bathroom remodel costs in Chicago average $15K–$20K for a full gut, which is right in line with national averages but don't let that fool you — material costs have leveled off from the 2022–2023 craziness but labor demand is still high. Book early.

### Pro Tip
**Splurge on waterproofing and tile work in bathrooms, save on the vanity.** A beautifully tiled shower with proper waterproofing will last 20+ years. That vanity? You can swap it out in 5 years when your taste changes. Also — spend on a good exhaust fan. Moisture destroys bathrooms. The $150 Panasonic WhisperCeiling is worth every penny over the $30 builder-grade fan.

## 💰💰💰 The $50K Budget: The Full Send

**Best for:** Homeowners who are staying put and want their space to actually work for them. This is investment-level renovation.

$50K in Chicago gets you into serious territory. We're talking the kind of remodel that changes how you live in your home.

### What $50K Gets You in Chicago

**Option A: Full Kitchen Gut Renovation ($35K–$50K)**

- Complete demo down to the studs
- New layout (within reason — moving gas lines and plumbing adds up fast)
- Semi-custom or custom cabinets (soft-close everything, dovetail drawers)
- Quartz or natural stone countertops
- Full tile backsplash (maybe that herringbone pattern you've been saving on Pinterest)
- New electrical — dedicated circuits for appliances, under-cabinet lighting, pendant lights over the island
- Plumbing updates
- New flooring throughout kitchen and adjacent areas
- *Appliances are typically separate — budget $3K–$8K on top for mid-range to high-end*

**Option B: Multi-Room Renovation**

- Full bathroom remodel ($15K–$18K)
- Kitchen refresh with new counters, backsplash, paint ($8K–$12K)
- New flooring throughout main level ($5K–$8K)
- Interior paint whole house ($3K–$5K)
- Basement finish-out or second bathroom ($10K–$15K)
- This is the "touch everything" approach — less dramatic per room but the whole house feels new

**Option C: High-End Single Space**

- Full kitchen with premium finishes — custom cabinetry, waterfall quartz island, statement lighting, professional-grade ventilation
- Or a primary suite renovation — spa bathroom with freestanding tub, frameless glass shower, heated floors, double vanity, walk-in closet build-out

### Realistic Timeline
**8–14 weeks** for a full kitchen gut. Multi-room projects can run 10–16 weeks depending on complexity. We phase the work so you're not living in a construction zone the entire time (though... it's still a construction zone. I won't lie to you).

### Chicago-Specific Context
A full kitchen remodel cost in Chicago averages $40K–$60K in 2026, depending on the neighborhood and the finishes. A bungalow kitchen in Portage Park is a different beast than a pre-war condo in Lincoln Park (condo rules, HOA approvals, freight elevator scheduling — it all adds up). Permitting for electrical and plumbing work is mandatory in Chicago and inspections are strict. That's actually a *good* thing — it means the work is done right.

### Pro Tip
**Splurge on layout and cabinetry, save on trendy finishes.** A well-designed kitchen layout lasts forever. That matte black faucet that's all over Instagram right now? The finish might wear in 3 years. Classic brushed nickel or chrome never goes out of style. And please — get soft-close hinges on everything. Your future self at 2 AM getting a snack will thank you.

Chicago requires licensed contractors for a reason. We pull permits, we pass inspections, and we warranty our work. It costs more upfront because it's done right. End of story.
