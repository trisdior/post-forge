## Breaking Down the Costs

### Tile ($2,000–$12,000)
Tile is usually the biggest visual impact in a bathroom. Costs vary wildly:

- **Ceramic/porcelain (floor):** $4–$10/sq ft installed
- **Subway tile (walls):** $5–$12/sq ft installed
- **Large format tile:** $8–$15/sq ft installed
- **Natural stone (marble, travertine):** $15–$30/sq ft installed
- **Custom mosaic/accent:** $20–$50/sq ft installed

A typical full bathroom has 80–120 sq ft of tile between floor and shower walls. At $8–$15/sq ft installed, that's $640–$1,800 just for tile labor and materials — before any fancy accents.

**Chicago tip:** Heated floors are popular here (our winters are brutal). Budget an extra $800–$1,500 for electric radiant floor heating in a standard bathroom.

### Vanity & Countertop ($800–$5,000)
- **Stock vanity (Home Depot/Lowes):** $300–$1,200
- **Semi-custom vanity:** $1,000–$3,000
- **Custom floating vanity:** $2,000–$5,000
- **Countertop (quartz):** $500–$1,500
- **Countertop (marble):** $800–$2,500

Double vanities for master baths run 40–60% more than single.

### Shower/Tub ($1,500–$10,000)
- **Tub-to-shower conversion:** $3,000–$6,000
- **Shower retile (existing footprint):** $2,000–$5,000
- **Custom tile shower with niche:** $4,000–$8,000
- **Frameless glass enclosure:** $1,200–$3,000
- **Freestanding tub:** $1,000–$5,000 (plus $500–$1,500 for plumbing relocation)

Tub-to-shower conversions are one of the most requested projects we see in Chicago — especially in older bungalows where the original cast iron tub has seen better days.

### Plumbing ($1,500–$5,000)
- **Fixture swap (same location):** $500–$1,500
- **Relocating fixtures:** $2,000–$5,000
- **New plumbing rough-in:** $3,000–$6,000

**Chicago-specific:** Many older Chicago homes have galvanized steel pipes. If your home was built before 1960, there's a good chance your pipes need replacing. Budget $2,000–$5,000 extra if this comes up during demo.

### Electrical ($500–$2,000)
- **New GFCI outlets:** $150–$300 each
- **Exhaust fan (vented to exterior):** $300–$800
- **Recessed lighting:** $150–$300 per fixture
- **Heated floor wiring:** $800–$1,500

Chicago code requires GFCI protection on all bathroom outlets and proper ventilation. If your current bathroom has no exhaust fan (common in older homes), adding one is basically mandatory for a permitted remodel.

### Permits & Inspections ($300–$1,500)
Any bathroom remodel in Chicago that involves plumbing or electrical changes requires a permit. The city inspects rough-in work before you close walls. Budget $300–$1,500 depending on scope.

## What Affects Your Bathroom Remodel Cost the Most?

### 1. Moving Plumbing
Keeping the toilet, sink, and shower in their current locations saves thousands. Moving a toilet even a few feet can cost $1,000–$3,000 in plumbing alone.

### 2. Tile Selection
The difference between $4/sq ft ceramic and $25/sq ft marble — across 100 sq ft — is $2,100. Material choices compound fast in bathrooms.

### 3. Scope Creep
You opened the wall to retile and found water damage and mold. Now it's a $3,000 remediation before you can even start the remodel. This is extremely common in Chicago's older housing stock. **Always budget 10–15% contingency.**

### 4. Labor
Chicago labor rates for tile setters, plumbers, and electricians are above the national average. A skilled tile setter in Chicago charges $45–$75/hour. It's worth it — bad tile work is obvious and expensive to fix.

## Best ROI Bathroom Upgrades in Chicago

If you're on a budget, these give you the biggest bang for your buck:

1. **New vanity + mirror + lighting** ($1,500–$3,000) — Completely changes the look
2. **Retile the shower** ($2,000–$4,000) — The shower is the focal point
3. **New fixtures (faucet, showerhead, towel bars)** ($500–$1,500) — Brushed gold and matte black are trending hard
4. **Fresh paint + new hardware** ($300–$600) — The cheapest transformation
5. **Heated floors** ($800–$1,500) — A luxury that actually pays off in Chicago winters

## Timeline Reality Check

Bathroom remodels in Chicago take longer than you expect. Here's why:
- **Permit processing:** 2–10 business days
- **Material lead times:** Custom tile and vanities can take 2–6 weeks to arrive
- **Inspection scheduling:** City inspectors aren't always available next-day
- **Hidden issues:** Old plumbing, water damage, asbestos floor tile (common in pre-1980 homes)

**Plan for 3–6 weeks for a full bathroom remodel.** A simple refresh (paint, vanity, fixtures) can be done in a week.
