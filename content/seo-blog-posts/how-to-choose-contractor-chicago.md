Hiring a contractor in Chicago can feel like a gamble. Horror stories are everywhere — blown budgets, disappearing contractors, work that fails inspection. But it doesn't have to be that way.

Here's how to find a good contractor, what to watch out for, and how to protect yourself — straight from a Chicago GC.

## 10 Red Flags That Should Make You Walk Away

### 1. No License
In Illinois, general contractors don't need a state license — but **Chicago requires a General Contractor License** for work within city limits. If someone's doing work in Chicago without one, that's a problem. Always ask.

### 2. No Insurance
This is non-negotiable. Your contractor needs:
- **General liability insurance** ($1M minimum)
- **Workers' compensation** (if they have employees)

Ask for a certificate of insurance. Call the insurance company to verify it's active. If a worker gets hurt on your property and the contractor has no workers' comp, **you could be liable**.

### 3. They Want a Huge Deposit Upfront
Industry standard is 10–30% to get started — enough to order materials. If someone asks for 50% or more upfront before any work begins, that's a red flag.

**Good structure:**
- 10–20% at signing
- 30% at rough-in (framing, plumbing, electrical)
- 30% at finish work
- 10–20% at final walkthrough

### 4. No Written Contract
If it's not in writing, it doesn't exist. Your contract should include:
- Detailed scope of work
- Total price and payment schedule
- Timeline with start and completion dates
- Change order process
- Warranty information
- Permit responsibilities

### 5. They Won't Pull Permits
"We don't need a permit for this" is sometimes true — and often a lie. In Chicago, you need permits for:
- Structural changes
- Electrical work
- Plumbing changes
- HVAC modifications
- Window/door replacements (in some cases)
- Decks and porches

Unpermitted work can result in fines, forced demolition, and problems when you sell.

### 6. No References or Portfolio
A contractor who can't show you past work is a contractor you shouldn't hire. Ask for:
- 3+ references from the last 12 months
- Photos of completed projects
- Addresses you can drive by (exterior work)

### 7. The Price Is Way Too Low
If one bid is $15,000 and everyone else is $25,000–$30,000, that $15K bid isn't a deal — it's a warning. They're either cutting corners, underqualified, or planning to hit you with change orders later.

### 8. No Timeline or Vague Timeline
"We'll get it done in a few weeks" isn't a timeline. A good contractor gives you:
- A start date
- Milestone dates
- An estimated completion date
- Buffer for inspections and weather (this is Chicago, after all)

### 9. They Pressure You to Decide Now
"This price is only good today" is a sales tactic, not a construction practice. A good contractor lets you take their estimate home, compare it, and make a decision.

### 10. Bad Online Reviews (or No Reviews at All)
Check Google, Yelp, BBB, and Angi. Look for patterns:
- One bad review? Could be a fluke.
- Multiple complaints about the same thing (no-shows, cost overruns, sloppy work)? That's a pattern.
- Zero reviews? They're either brand new or operating under a different name than last time.

## What to Look For in a Good Chicago Contractor

✅ Licensed and insured (ask for proof)
✅ Clear, detailed written estimates
✅ Communicates well — returns calls, answers questions
✅ Portfolio of completed work in the Chicago area
✅ Pulls permits and schedules inspections
✅ Provides a written contract before work starts
✅ Doesn't ask for more than 30% upfront
✅ Has a physical business presence (not just a cell phone)
✅ Offers a workmanship warranty (1 year minimum)
✅ Is willing to walk you through the process

## How to Verify a Chicago Contractor

1. **Check their license:** [Chicago BACP License Search](https://webapps1.chicago.gov/activegcl/)
2. **Verify insurance:** Ask for a Certificate of Insurance and call the provider
3. **Check BBB:** [bbb.org](https://www.bbb.org)
4. **Google them:** Search "[Company Name] + reviews" and "[Company Name] + complaints"
5. **Ask your alderman's office:** They sometimes know which contractors have open violations

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring

1. How long have you been in business?
2. Are you licensed to work in Chicago?
3. Can I see your insurance certificate?
4. Who will be on-site daily managing the project?
5. How do you handle change orders?
6. What's your warranty on workmanship?
7. Will you pull all necessary permits?
8. What's your payment schedule?
9. Can I talk to your last 3 clients?
10. What happens if the project goes over budget or timeline?

## The Bottom Line

Hiring a contractor is one of the biggest financial decisions you'll make as a homeowner. Take your time, do your homework, and trust your gut. A good contractor will make the process easy — a bad one will make it a nightmare.
