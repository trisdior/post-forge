If you're a Chicago homeowner thinking about remodeling your kitchen, the first question is always the same: *how much is this going to cost me?*

The answer depends on the scope, materials, and who you hire — but here's what real kitchen remodels are running in the Chicago area in 2026.

## Quick Cost Breakdown

| Remodel Level | Typical Range | What's Included |
|---|---|---|
| **Budget Refresh** | $8,000–$18,000 | Paint, hardware, countertops, backsplash |
| **Mid-Range Remodel** | $25,000–$55,000 | New cabinets, countertops, flooring, appliances |
| **Full Gut Renovation** | $60,000–$120,000+ | Layout changes, plumbing, electrical, custom everything |

These are realistic Chicago-area numbers — not national averages that don't account for our labor and permit costs.

## What Drives Kitchen Remodel Costs in Chicago?

### 1. Cabinets (30–40% of Budget)
Cabinets are the single biggest line item. Stock cabinets from a big box store run $3,000–$8,000. Semi-custom from a local supplier: $8,000–$20,000. Full custom: $20,000–$50,000+.

**Pro tip:** Refacing existing cabinets can save 40–60% if the boxes are still solid. We see a lot of solid oak cabinets from the '90s in Chicago homes that just need new doors and hardware.

### 2. Countertops
- **Laminate:** $800–$2,000
- **Quartz:** $3,000–$7,000
- **Granite:** $2,500–$6,000
- **Marble:** $5,000–$12,000

Quartz has been the go-to in Chicago for the last few years. Low maintenance, consistent look, and the price has come down.

### 3. Labor Costs
Chicago labor runs higher than the national average — expect $50–$85/hour for skilled tradespeople. A full remodel typically involves:
- General contractor
- Electrician (Chicago requires licensed electricians for permit work)
- Plumber
- Tile installer
- Painter

**Important:** Chicago requires permits for most kitchen remodels, especially if you're moving plumbing or electrical. Budget $500–$2,000 for permits and inspections.

### 4. Appliances
- **Budget package (4 pieces):** $2,500–$4,500
- **Mid-range (Samsung, LG, KitchenAid):** $5,000–$10,000
- **Premium (Sub-Zero, Wolf, Thermador):** $15,000–$40,000+

### 5. Flooring
- **Luxury vinyl plank:** $3–$7/sq ft installed
- **Tile:** $6–$15/sq ft installed
- **Hardwood:** $8–$15/sq ft installed

For a typical 150 sq ft Chicago kitchen, flooring runs $1,000–$2,500.

## Chicago-Specific Factors That Affect Your Cost

**Older homes:** Many Chicago homes (especially bungalows, two-flats, and greystones) have outdated wiring and plumbing. If your home was built before 1970, budget an extra $3,000–$8,000 for bringing systems up to code.

**Condo remodels:** If you're in a condo, your HOA likely has rules about construction hours, contractor insurance requirements, and material delivery. Some buildings require a renovation deposit ($5,000–$25,000). Factor this into your timeline and budget.

**Seasonal pricing:** Chicago contractors are busiest from April through October. Scheduling your remodel in late fall or winter can sometimes save 10–15% on labor.

## How to Get the Best Value on Your Chicago Kitchen Remodel

1. **Get 3+ quotes** — Prices vary significantly between contractors. Make sure quotes include the same scope of work.
2. **Don't skip the permit** — Unpermitted work can kill a home sale and create liability issues.
3. **Prioritize the layout** — Moving the sink or stove adds $3,000–$8,000 in plumbing/electrical. Keep things where they are if possible.
4. **Invest in what you touch** — Spend on hardware, faucets, and countertops. Save on things behind walls.
5. **Hire local** — A contractor who knows Chicago building codes, inspectors, and suppliers will save you headaches.
