## 🟠 Reddit Post (r/chicago or r/homeimprovement)

**Title:** Honest cost breakdown: What $10K, $25K, and $50K actually gets you in a Chicago remodel (2026)

**Body:**

I'm a GC in Chicago and I see a lot of posts asking about remodel costs, so I figured I'd share some real numbers from projects we do regularly. These are 2026 Chicago prices — not national averages.

**$10K range:**
- Full interior paint (3bd/1ba, ~1,200 sq ft): $3,000–$4,500 with quality paint (BM, SW)
- LVP flooring for main areas (500–800 sq ft): $3,000–$5,000 installed
- Bathroom refresh (new vanity, fixtures, mirror, paint — keeping existing tub/tile): $1,500–$2,500
- Handyman punch list (hardware, outlets, drywall patches): $500–$1,500
- Timeline: 2–3 weeks

**$25K range:**
- Full bathroom gut: $12K–$18K (tiled shower, new vanity, toilet, proper waterproofing with Kerdi/RedGard, cement board — not green board)
- Partial kitchen (new cabinets or reface, quartz counters at $50–$75/sq ft, backsplash, fixtures): $15K–$22K
- Basement finish (~800 sq ft, framing/drywall/LVP/lighting): $20K–$25K depending on bathroom and egress
- Timeline: 4–8 weeks + Chicago permit delays

**$50K range:**
- Full kitchen gut to studs: $35K–$50K (semi-custom cabinets, quartz, tile backsplash, new electrical/plumbing, flooring). Appliances are separate, budget $3K–$8K.
- Multi-room approach: bathroom + kitchen refresh + floors + paint + basement = touch everything for less drama per room
- Timeline: 8–14 weeks for kitchen, 10–16 for multi-room

**Where to splurge vs save:**
- Splurge: waterproofing, paint quality, cabinet construction, shower valves
- Save: vanities (easy to swap later), light fixtures (tons of good options under $100), trendy finishes (matte black everything will date itself)

**Chicago-specific notes:**
- Labor runs 15–20% higher than suburbs
- Permits are mandatory for electrical/plumbing and inspections are strict (good thing)
- Old homes = surprises behind walls. Always have a contingency budget (10–15%)
- Condo remodels add complexity: HOA rules, elevator scheduling, neighbor notifications

Happy to answer questions. Not trying to sell anything here — just sharing real numbers since I see a lot of wildly inaccurate estimates floating around.
