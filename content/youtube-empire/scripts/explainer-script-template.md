## WRITING PRINCIPLES

1. **One analogy per concept.** If you can't explain it with an analogy, you don't understand it well enough.
2. **Specificity > Vagueness.** "10,800°F" hits harder than "really hot." "Dr. Sarah Chen at MIT" hits harder than "scientists."
3. **Build a rollercoaster.** Curiosity → Understanding → Surprise → Wonder. Repeat.
4. **Every 30 seconds needs a new visual.** If you're writing 3+ sentences without a [VISUAL] note, you're losing people.
5. **The "tell a friend" test.** If the viewer can't explain the video's main point to a friend in one sentence, you failed.
6. **End on wonder, not answers.** The best explainers make people MORE curious, not less.
7. **Cut the throat-clearing.** No "Hey guys, welcome back." Start with the content.
